Real struggles learners face

How many of these
sound familiar?

👂
Listening
You hear it, but don't understand it
  • You recognize a word's sound but miss the full meaning
  • Content you've studied still sounds unfamiliar from a native speaker
  • You speak, but the moment they reply, you're lost
🗣️
Speaking
You can say it, but not well
  • You know the words but can't form a full sentence
  • Unsure of tones and pronunciation, so you hesitate to speak
  • You speak, but the other person looks confused, and you don't know why
  • Even after speaking, you can't tell if it came out right
📖
Comprehension
You read the words, but miss the meaning
  • You know each word, but together they make no sense
  • Dictionary meanings feel too formal for real conversation
  • You can't tell if your phrasing sounds natural to locals
🙋
Practice
You want to speak, but have no one to practice with
  • No one around to actually practice real conversation with
  • Mistakes go uncorrected, and bad habits pile up
  • Self-study progress stalls with no clear way forward

Learning progress

Most students start speaking
around this point

1
Lessons 1–5
Building the tone system
Understand Thai tone logic, produce basic syllables correctly, and say your first sentence a Thai person can understand
2
Lessons 5–10
Mastering sentence structure
Understand Thai sentence structure, learn 5–10 core grammar concepts, and build full sentences
3
Lessons 10–15
Time, negation, and questions
Use time expressions, negative sentences, and questions — a big jump in everyday communication coverage
4
Lesson 16 onward
Developing a feel for the language
Start feeling the language instead of memorizing it, guess unfamiliar words on your own, and genuinely think in Thai

This is an average pace — actual progress varies, and weekly review after class makes a big difference.

Pronunciation · Lesson 1
The first phrases you'll learn

sà-wàt-dii khráp / khâ
Hello (men say khráp, women say khâ)
ขอบคุณครับ / ค่ะ
khɔ̀ɔp-khun khráp / khâ
Thank you
Tone system · Core lesson-1 concept
Thai tones aren't hard — they follow rules
กิน · ไข่ · ข้าว
gin · khài · khâao
eat · egg · rice — get the tone right, and communication just works

✦ The biggest misconception Taiwanese learners have is that spelling rules are only for reading and writing — really, they're the key to understanding how Thai is actually pronounced.

Sentence structure · Stage 2
Learning to build sentences, not just words
Subject + grammar marker + verb + object
เราอยากไปเที่ยวเมืองไทย (I want to go visit Thailand)
Once you understand the structure, you can actually start speaking
